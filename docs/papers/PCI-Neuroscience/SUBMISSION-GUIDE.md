# PCI Neuroscience + Peer Community Journal — Submission Guide

Source: peercommunityin.org / neuro.peercommunityin.org (guide_for_authors returned 404 at
time of setup — verify current URLs before submitting).

## What this venue is

Peer Community In (PCI) Neuroscience is a non-profit that provides free, open, named peer
review of preprints. A recommended preprint can then be published in Peer Community Journal
(PCJ), which is diamond OA (free to read, free to publish, no APC).

## Cost

Free. No APC for authors or readers.

## Process (two steps)

1. Post preprint to bioRxiv or arXiv (no charge). The preprint must be publicly accessible.
2. Submit to PCI Neuroscience at neuro.peercommunityin.org. A volunteer handling editor
   coordinates 2-3 rounds of open, named peer review. Reviews and the recommendation letter
   are posted publicly.
3. If the handling editor recommends: optionally submit the recommended version to Peer
   Community Journal (PCJ) for formal publication with DOI, or to any PCI-friendly journal.
   PCJ publishes any PCI-recommended preprint as-is.

## Paper format

No specific template. PCI accepts preprints in any standard format (PDF). For PCJ, the
recommended preprint version becomes the published version; no reformatting required.

## Recommended manuscript structure for this paper

- Abstract (~250 words)
- Introduction / Statement of need
- Architecture / Methods
- Results / Evaluation (full benchmark tables allowed — no word limit on methods papers)
- Discussion / Limitations
- Acknowledgements
- References

## Scope fit

PCI Neuroscience covers computational neuroscience, neuroinformatics, and tools for neuroscience
research. A streaming preprocessing engine for Neuropixels-scale recordings fits. The community
skews toward biological neuroscience; email the managing board before submitting to confirm
a handling editor is available for software/systems work.

## Turnaround

Variable: 2–6 months from submission to recommendation, depending on editor and reviewer
availability. PCJ publishes within days of recommendation.

## Key notes

- Reviews are public and named — reviewers see each other's reviews.
- Preprint must not be under review at another peer-reviewed journal simultaneously.
- The AGPL-3.0 license is compatible; PCI does not impose license requirements on software.
- Registered Reports track is available but not relevant for this paper type.
